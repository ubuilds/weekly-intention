# Vision

Weekly Intention is a minimal app that holds a single intention for the current week and keeps it gently present across every device I use.

It embodies orientation over productivity — presence over execution, visibility without pressure.

---

## Purpose

Most tools push me to do more, track more, optimize more. Weekly Intention does the opposite. It asks for one thing — what matters this week — and then steps out of the way, keeping that single choice quietly visible on every screen I look at, so the week has a direction without becoming a checklist.

---

## What it does for me

- Holds one intention for the current calendar week — editable any time, with no penalty for changing my mind.
- Keeps that intention identical on every surface — iPhone, Mac, home-screen and lock-screen widgets, and Apple Watch — all converged to the same value.
- Lets me look back: past intentions stay recallable and searchable, with no scores, streaks, or judgement attached.

It is built for **orienting**, not for performing.

---

## Principles

**One intention, one week.**
Exactly one current intention exists per calendar week, and it is the same everywhere. No multiple intentions, no versioning surfaced to me.

**Convergence is foundational.**
Every surface — phone, Mac, widget, watch — resolves to the same value. Sync is not a feature; it is the floor the whole app stands on. Last write wins; temporary divergence is acceptable, permanent divergence is not.

**Minimal.**
Small surface area. Few features. Each one earns its place.
Cleanup and refactoring are part of the work, not extra.

**Action-oriented.**
The app helps me stay pointed at what matters this week.
It does not nag, gamify, or moralize.

**Honest.**
What I see reflects what is true.
No smoothing, no hidden defaults.

**Low-frequency by design.**
The app earns the open by being useful on arrival, not by pulling me back.
No notifications, no streaks, no engagement loops.

**Changing my mind is not failure.**
Swapping the intention mid-week is the app working as intended, not a lapse.
It holds what I choose; it never grades whether I lived up to it.

**Secure by default.**
Treated with the care the data deserves.
My intentions live in my own iCloud account, never in the repo.

---

## Long horizon

For now: just for me.

If it stays mine, that is enough.

If it grows into something others would value, the door is open.
A bridge I cross only if I want to.

I do not force it. I let it emerge.

---

## How I build it

In the spirit of my Independent Practice:

- ship small
- reflect after each release
- cleanup is shipping too
- one mode at a time
- rest is allowed

The goal is not a more impressive app.
The goal is a tool that helps me stay oriented to what matters this week — and a way of building that stays alive.
